Engineered Hardwood vs Solid Hardwood: Which One Should You Choose?

Expert flooring advice from Abeln Floor Systems, St. Louis

Understanding Engineered Hardwood

Engineered hardwood consists of multiple layers of wood, with a real hardwood veneer on top. It is more stable than solid hardwood in areas with humidity changes and can often be installed over concrete or radiant heating. Understanding Solid Hardwood

Solid hardwood is made from a single piece of wood, offering timeless beauty and durability. It can be sanded and refinished multiple times, making it perfect for long-term use. However, it is more sensitive to moisture and temperature changes, so proper installation by professionals is crucial.

Cost Comparison

Engineered hardwood is usually slightly less expensive than solid hardwood, but pricing depends on wood species, plank thickness, and finish. Benefits of Choosing the Right Hardwood

Both options increase property value, improve indoor air quality, and add elegance to your home. Engineered hardwood offers flexibility in installation, while solid hardwood offers unmatched longevity.
